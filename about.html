About Signal & Noise

◆

Who writes this thing

I'm Derek. I trade by day, build by night, and I'm a dad of 7. This newsletter is how I make sense of the markets — and share what I see.

I've been trading options for years. Not in a hedge fund. Not on a prop desk. I trade from my desk at home between meetings, school pickups, and too many cups of coffee. And I write about it because most market commentary sounds like it was generated by a machine wearing a suit.

What this is

A weekly markets newsletter in plain English. No jargon, no hype, just honest analysis. Every Sunday evening, you get one email with everything that mattered in the markets that week — and what I think is coming next.

I'm not going to tell you the Fed is "maintaining a hawkish posture amid tightening financial conditions." I'm going to tell you Powell said chill, rates aren't moving, and here's what that means for your trades.

Why it exists

Most market newsletters sound like they were written by a Bloomberg terminal. Cold. Jargon-heavy. Written for people who already know everything. That's not useful.

I write like I'm explaining it to a friend. Because that's exactly what this is — me, telling you what I see, what I'm trading, and what I got wrong. No pretending. No guru act. Just signal. Less noise.

What you get every week

The Lead

The biggest market story of the week, broken down in human English. What happened, why it matters, and what comes next.

The Tuition Report

A real trade I made that week. Entry, exit, result. What I learned — especially from the losses. No cherry-picking.

Trade Ideas

Specific setups I'm watching. Entries, targets, risk/reward. Free readers get one. Signal Pro readers get them all.

Quick Hits

The tickers that moved, the numbers that matter, and one-line takes on each. Fast, scannable, useful.
