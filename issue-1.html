Lead Story

AI Just Ate $1 Trillion of Software Value. In a Week.

Anthropic dropped a plugin. Wall Street dropped software stocks off a cliff.

◆

Last Friday, Anthropic launched new plugins for Claude Cowork — legal research, marketing analysis, data analytics. Stuff that used to require a Salesforce license or a Bloomberg terminal. Now it's a plugin. The market's reaction was biblical.

Software stocks entered a bear market. Cloud Computing Fund down 20% YTD. The sector's forward P/E compressed from 33.1x to 23.2x — a 30% valuation haircut in six days. Reuters tallied the damage: nearly $1 trillion wiped from software stocks since Jan 28. They're calling it the "SaaS-pocalypse."

But here's what nobody's connecting: Silver had its largest daily crash since 1980 (down 26%). Gold dropped 9%, worst since 2013. First U.S. bank of 2026 failed. Job openings hit lowest since COVID. January layoffs hit highest since the 2009 financial crisis. Kevin Warsh nominated to replace Powell. All in one week.

This isn't a tech correction. This is the market repricing what the future looks like when AI actually works. Google announced $175-185B in 2026 capex, Amazon followed with $200B. Companies building AI infrastructure are spending like it's the Manhattan Project. Companies whose products AI replaces are getting destroyed.

When the dust settles, we'll look back at this week as the moment Wall Street stopped treating AI as a story and started treating it as a restructuring.

Sources: Reuters, CNBC, Business Insider, The Guardian, CBS News

Lesson Macro > Technicals

NVDA gapped down, RSI was oversold at 22. The playbook says buy cheap calls and ride the bounce. So I did. Three contracts at $0.96 each.

Problem? When the entire sector is rotating out, "oversold" can get a lot more oversold. RSI said bounce. Macro said run. Macro wins. Every time.

The lesson: $288 lost is tuition, not a funeral. Save big bets for when macro and technicals agree. When they're fighting each other, sit on your hands or go small.

Analysis

Deep Dive: What's Actually Happening With AI Spending

Google: $175-185B on AI in 2026. Amazon: $200B. The entire GDP of Hungary is ~$200B. Amazon is spending Hungary-money on chips in one year.

The market has two opposite reactions simultaneously. One: "AI is real. Buy infrastructure builders." Two: "If AI works, what happens to software companies charging $50/month for something Claude does free? Sell SaaS." Both are rational. That's why it's chaos.

Broadcom (builds AI chips) getting dragged down alongside ServiceNow (sells software AI might replace). Like selling both the arsonist and the fire department.

What people miss: every dollar Google spends is a dollar Broadcom, Nvidia, or TSMC earns. Every data center needs power, cooling, real estate. This is the biggest capital deployment since the railroads. Some companies will be the Union Pacific. Others will be the horse-and-buggy.

Software correction is overdone short-term — 30% P/E compression in a week is panic, not precision. But the direction is right.

The winners: Companies that BUILD AI infrastructure. Companies that USE AI to get better (not replaced). Whatever's left standing.

We're in a once-in-a-generation repricing. It's messy. Volatile. And it's where fortunes get made — if you're paying attention.
